I am a freshmen at UC Riverside majoring in Education, Society and Human Development. Teaching has been a dream of mine from a young age and devoted myself to this major in hopes of getting my credentials in elementary school teaching. Coming from a low income community and seeing all the teachers hoping to help students reach higher education made me think of the rewarding feeling when we see said students succeed. As a teacher I desire to encourage students to follow the path they desire and give them the skills they would need and give them the comfort zone my teachers provided for me.

The University of California, Riverside provided the right resources for me to continue my journey to my dream job. UCR is known for a small yet amazing School of Education. It gives an option of getting both a masters' degree and teaching credentials at once. This university also happens to be Hispanic dominant which helped me connect with many people with similar backgrounds with the same goal of wanting to impact young children into better education opportunities for a better future.

UCR has so far been a great community and facility to study in. I hope to graduate in 4 years with my bachelor's and then apply to the credential program and come out with both a masters' and teaching credential. After college, I hope to settle as a teacher in my community as I want to give children like me to have a chance of being taught with someone who understands them and wants to see them succeed.
